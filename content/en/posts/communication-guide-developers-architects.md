[Communication Guide for Developers and Architects - Learning Information Transmission through Patterns](https://amzn.to/3ZcZy5P) is a book that discusses various aspects of communication, including visual information, text, verbal and non-verbal communication, and documentation.

Personally, I felt that the title could also be interpreted as a documentation guide for software engineers.

The content is easy to read, and it can be finished in a few hours.

I didn't gain much new information, but I believe the topics covered are good to have as a common understanding among those involved in software development.

I think it would be a suitable recommendation for organizations and teams.

Since the uncertainty and instability of communication can affect group performance, it would be beneficial to use this book as a guide.

Three points that left an impression on me are:

- The content takes diversity into account.
- A decent amount of pages are dedicated to ADR.
- It touches on remote work (although returning to the office seems to be a trend lately...).

While the content feels aimed at juniors, even someone with a certain level of experience like myself realized that there are aspects I understand but do not execute well, and that was enlightening. (Especially regarding my presentation materials...)
