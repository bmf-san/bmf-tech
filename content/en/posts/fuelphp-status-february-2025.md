Regarding FuelPHP 2.0, there were initial plans to support **PHP7.4 and above but below 8.0** ([bmf-tech.com - Current Status of FuelPHP as of March 2023](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/FuelPHP%E3%81%AE2023%E5%B9%B43%E6%9C%88%E7%8F%BE%E5%9C%A8%E3%81%AE%E7%8F%BE%E6%B3%81#:~:text=Forum%E3%82%84commit%E3%82%92%E8%A6%8B%E3%82%8B%E9%99%90%E3%82%8A%E3%80%81PHP8)), but due to delays in development, this policy is also fluid. It is likely that by the time the official version of 2.0 is released, support for PHP8 series (preferably 8.2 and beyond) will be required, but details are undecided.

## Security Support Information
The FuelPHP project has **not officially announced support deadlines or EOL**. Information such as "how long security updates will be provided" or "when EOL will be" is not specified, and at least for the 1 series, it is considered to be **effectively unsupported** ([bmf-tech.com - Current Status of FuelPHP as of March 2023](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/FuelPHP%E3%81%AE2023%E5%B9%B43%E6%9C%88%E7%8F%BE%E5%9C%A8%E3%81%AE%E7%8F%BE%E6%B3%81#:~:text=Forum%E3%81%AB%E3%82%88%E3%82%8B%E3%81%A8%E8%A8%AD%E8%A8%88%E3%81%8C%E5%88%B7%E6%96%B0%E3%81%95%E3%82%8C%E3%81%9F%E3%83%90%E3%83%BC%E3%82%B8%E3%83%A7%E3%83%B3%E3%81%A7%E3%80%81PHP%E3%81%AF7)). As mentioned earlier, developers themselves have acknowledged that "the 1 series has already passed EOL" ([bmf-tech.com - Current Status of FuelPHP as of March 2023](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/FuelPHP%E3%81%AE2023%E5%B9%B43%E6%9C%88%E7%8F%BE%E5%9C%A8%E3%81%AE%E7%8F%BE%E6%B3%81#:~:text=%E4%BC%9A%E8%A9%B1%E3%81%AE%E3%82%84%E3%82%8A%E3%81%A8%E3%82%8A%E3%81%A7%E7%9F%A5%E3%81%A3%E3%81%9F%E3%81%93%E3%81%A8%E3%81%A0%E3%81%8C%E3%80%81FuelPHP1%E7%B3%BB%E3%81%AF%E6%97%A2%E3%81%ABEOL%E3%82%92%E9%81%8E%E3%81%8E%E3%81%A6%E3%81%84%E3%82%8B%E3%82%89%E3%81%97%E3%81%84%E3%80%82%E3%81%9D%E3%82%8C%E6%95%85%E3%81%8B1%E7%B3%BB%E3%81%AB%E3%81%AF%E3%82%82%E3%81%AF%E3%82%84%E6%B3%A8%E5%8A%9B%E3%81%97%E3%81%A6%E3%81%84%E3%81%8B%E3%81%AA%E3%81%84%E6%96%B9%E9%87%9D%E3%81%A0%E3%81%A8%E3%81%84%E3%81%86%E3%81%93%E3%81%A8%E3%82%82%E8%81%9E%E3%81%84%E3%81%9F%E3%80%82)), and the official team has little intention of providing active security fixes for the 1 series.

However, since FuelPHP itself is community-driven open-source, there is a possibility that **patches will be provided by volunteers or major developers if serious security vulnerabilities are found**. In fact, developers who continue to use FuelPHP internally have been applying bug fixes and updates themselves, and in some cases, they implicitly maintain the framework by sending PRs to GitHub ([bmf-tech.com - Current Status of FuelPHP as of March 2023](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/FuelPHP%E3%81%AE2023%E5%B9%B43%E6%9C%88%E7%8F%BE%E5%9C%A8%E3%81%AE%E7%8F%BE%E6%B3%81#:~:text=Q,%E3%83%88%E3%81%95%E3%82%8C%E3%81%A6%E3%81%84%E3%82%8B%E3%80%82%E3%83%AA%E3%82%BD%E3%83%BC%E3%82%B9%E3%81%8C%E8%B6%B3%E3%82%8A%E3%81%AA%E3%81%84%E3%82%8F%E3%81%91%E3%81%A7%E3%81%AF%E3%81%AA%E3%81%84%E3%80%82)). However, since there is no official declaration of long-term support, **continuing to rely on FuelPHP 1 series poses a risk from a security perspective**. As of 2025, if you use FuelPHP 1 series, you should recognize that updates to the framework itself are almost not expected, and if necessary, you should take measures such as forking it yourself and applying security patches.

## Community Trends
The FuelPHP community has **significantly shrunk** compared to its peak. As a trend in PHP frameworks, Laravel is overwhelmingly popular, and there are fewer cases of new adoption of FuelPHP ([bmf-tech.com - Current Status of FuelPHP as of March 2023](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/FuelPHP%E3%81%AE2023%E5%B9%B43%E6%9C%88%E7%8F%BE%E5%9C%A8%E3%81%AE%E7%8F%BE%E6%B3%81#:~:text=2023%E5%B9%B43%E6%9C%88%E7%8F%BE%E5%9C%A8%E3%81%AEFuelPHP%E3%81%AE%E7%8F%BE%E6%B3%81%E3%81%AB%E3%81%A4%E3%81%84%E3%81%A6%E3%81%96%E3%81%A3%E3%81%8F%E3%82%8A%E8%AA%BF%E3%81%B9%E3%81%9F%E3%81%93%E3%81%A8%E3%82%92%E3%81%BE%E3%81%A8%E3%82%81%E3%82%8B%E3%80%82%20%E7%8B%AC%E8%87%AA%E3%81%AB%E8%AA%BF%E3%81%B9%E3%81%9F%E6%83%85%E5%A0%B1%E3%81%AA%E3%81%AE%E3%81%A7%E6%AD%A3%E7%A2%BA%E6%80%A7%E3%81%AB%E3%81%AF%E6%AC%A0%E3%81%91%E3%82%8B%E3%81%8B%E3%82%82%E3%81%97%E3%82%8C%E3%81%AA%E3%81%84%E3%80%82)). Overseas developers also express harsh evaluations such as "development is stagnant, and the community is practically dead" ([Is FuelPHP still a thing? : r/PHP - Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/PHP/comments/7xvfde/is_fuelphp_still_a_thing/#:~:text=Is%20FuelPHP%20still%20a%20thing%3F,developing%20on%20at%20this)), and the number of active users is decreasing. As a result, the ecosystem's excitement is limited, and interactions on official forums and GitHub are not frequent.

However, **the existing FuelPHP user community has not completely disappeared**. Companies and developers who have been operating legacy systems built with FuelPHP for many years still exist, and information sharing and support are conducted among those users ([bmf-tech.com - Current Status of FuelPHP as of March 2023](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/FuelPHP%E3%81%AE2023%E5%B9%B43%E6%9C%88%E7%8F%BE%E5%9C%A8%E3%81%AE%E7%8F%BE%E6%B3%81#:~:text=PHP%E3%81%AE%E3%83%95%E3%83%AC%E3%83%BC%E3%83%A0%E3%83%AF%E3%83%BC%E3%82%AF%E3%81%AFLaravel%E3%81%8C%E5%9C%A7%E5%80%92%E7%9A%84%E3%81%AB%E4%BA%BA%E6%B0%97%E3%81%8C%E3%81%82%E3%82%8B%E3%82%88%E3%81%86%E3%81%AB%E6%84%9F%E3%81%98%E3%82%8B%E3%81%8C%E3%80%81FuelPHP%E3%82%82%E3%81%BE%E3%81%A0%E7%8F%BE%E5%BD%B9%E3%81%A7%E4%BD%BF%E3%81%A3%E3%81%A6%E3%81%84%E3%82%8B%E4%BA%BA%E3%81%8C%E3%81%84%E3%82%8B%E3%81%8B%E3%82%82%E3%81%97%E3%82%8C%E3%81%AA%E3%81%84%E3%81%AE%E3%81%A7%E3%80%81%E4%BD%95%E3%81%8B%E4%B8%80%E5%8A%A9%E3%81%AB%E3%81%AA%E3%82%8C%E3%81%B0%E5%AC%89%E3%81%97%E3%81%84%E3%80%82)). For example, in Japan, there are blog articles about updating FuelPHP applications to PHP8 environments and information dissemination summarizing the current status of FuelPHP ([bmf-tech.com - Current Status of FuelPHP as of March 2023](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/FuelPHP%E3%81%AE2023%E5%B9%B43%E6%9C%88%E7%8F%BE%E5%9C%A8%E3%81%AE%E7%8F%BE%E6%B3%81#:~:text=%E6%A6%82%E8%A6%81)). Additionally, in the official forum, there were reports and questions about issues when migrating to PHP8.1 using the FuelPHP1.9 development version in 2024 ([Image driver error in PHP8.1 with 1.9 Develop? - FuelPHP forums](https://fuelphp.com/forums/discussion/15453/image-driver-error-in-php8-1-with-1-9-develop/p1#:~:text=forums%20fuelphp,Most)), and developers responded and addressed these issues. The main developer Harro also seems to continue providing **modest support** by responding to questions on the forum and committing necessary patches ([bmf-tech.com - Current Status of FuelPHP as of March 2023](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/FuelPHP%E3%81%AE2023%E5%B9%B43%E6%9C%88%E7%8F%BE%E5%9C%A8%E3%81%AE%E7%8F%BE%E6%B3%81#:~:text=Q.%201.9)).

In summary, **the FuelPHP community is small but still exists**. Although it is outside the trend of new development, mutual assistance and information exchange among existing users continue, and it has not reached a complete "project extinction." However, there is little prospect of an increase in the number of users in the future, and the main concern of the community is shifting to topics such as "how to migrate and update existing systems from FuelPHP" ([bmf-tech.com - Current Status of FuelPHP as of March 2023](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/FuelPHP%E3%81%AE2023%E5%B9%B43%E6%9C%88%E7%8F%BE%E5%9C%A8%E3%81%AE%E7%8F%BE%E6%B3%81#:~:text=FuelPHP%E3%81%AB%E3%83%AD%E3%83%83%E3%82%AF%E3%82%A4%E3%83%B3%E3%81%97%E3%81%A6%E3%81%84%E3%82%8B%E3%82%A2%E3%83%97%E3%83%AA%E3%82%B1%E3%83%BC%E3%82%B7%E3%83%A7%E3%83%B3%E3%82%92%E3%81%A9%E3%81%AE%E3%82%88%E3%81%86%E3%81%AB%E5%88%B7%E6%96%B0%E3%81%97%E3%81%A6%E3%81%84%E3%81%8F%E3%81%8B%E3%81%A8%E3%81%84%E3%81%86%E3%81%93%E3%81%A8%E3%81%AB%E8%AA%B2%E9%A1%8C%E3%82%92%E6%8C%81%E3%81%A3%E3%81%A6%E3%81%84%E3%82%8B%E3%82%B5%E3%83%BC%E3%83%93%E3%82%B9%E3%81%AF%E3%81%8D%E3%81%A3%E3%81%A8%E4%B8%96%E3%81%AE%E4%B8%AD%E3%81%BE%E3%81%A0%E4%B8%80%E6%9D%AF%E3%81%82%E3%82%8B%E3%81%A8%E6%80%9D%E3%81%86%E3%81%8C%E3%80%81%E5%90%84%E6%89%80%E3%81%A9%E3%81%86%E3%81%84%E3%81%86%20%E6%88%A6%E7%95%A5%E3%82%92%E5%8F%96%E3%81%A3%E3%81%A6%E3%81%84%E3%82%8B%E3%81%A0%E3%82%8D%E3%81%86%E3%81%8B%E3%83%BB%E3%83%BB%E6%B0%97%E3%81%AB%E3%81%AA%E3%82%8B%E3%80%82%20%E7%89%B9%E3%81%AB%E5%88%A5%E3%81%AE%E3%82%A2%E3%83%97%E3%83%AA%E3%82%B1%E3%83%BC%E3%82%B7%E3%83%A7%E3%83%B3%E3%80%81%E3%82%A2%E3%83%BC%E3%82%AD%E3%83%86%E3%82%AF%E3%83%81%E3%83%A3%E3%81%AB%E3%83%AA%E3%83%97%E3%83%AC%E3%82%A4%E3%82%B9%E3%81%99%E3%82%8B%E3%81%93%E3%81%A8%E3%81%8C%E7%8F%BE%E5%AE%9F%E7%9A%84%E3%81%A7%E3%81%AF%E3%81%AA%E3%81%84%E3%80%81%E3%81%8B%E3%81%AA%E3%82%8A%E3%81%AE%E7%97%9B%E3%81%BF%E3%82%92%E4%BC%B4%E3%81%86%E3%81%A8%E3%81%84%E3%81%A3%E3%81%9F%E5%A0%B4%E5%90%88%E3%81%AB%E3%81%8A%E3%81%84%E3%81%A6%E3%80%81%E5%8F%96%E3%82%8B%E3%81%B9%E3%81%8D%E9%81%B8%E6%8A%9E%E3%81%AF%E7%B5%90%E6%A7%8B%E9%99%90%E3%82%89%E3%82%8C%E3%82%8B%E3%81%AF%E3%81%9A%E3%83%BB%E3%83%BB%E3%83%BB%E3%80%82)).

## Official Announcements and Forum Discussions
The blog updates on the official site have long been suspended, and new official announcements are limited. The most recent clear information from the development team is the **commitment to FuelPHP 2.0 development**. In early 2023, an announcement was made on the FuelPHP forum and official Twitter stating "development of the 1.x series is frozen, and we will focus on 2.0 from now on" ([bmf-tech.com - Current Status of FuelPHP as of March 2023](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/FuelPHP%E3%81%AE2023%E5%B9%B43%E6%9C%88%E7%8F%BE%E5%9C%A8%E3%81%AE%E7%8F%BE%E6%B3%81#:~:text=%E3%81%A1%E3%82%87%E3%81%86%E3%81%A9%E3%81%9D%E3%82%93%E3%81%AA%E3%82%84%E3%82%8A%E3%81%A8%E3%82%8A%E3%82%92%E3%81%97%E3%81%A6%E3%81%84%E3%82%8B%E5%BE%8C%E6%97%A5%E3%80%81Forum%E3%82%84Twitter%E3%81%AB%E6%8A%95%E7%A8%BF%E3%81%8C%E3%81%82%E3%82%8A%E3%80%81%E3%81%A9%E3%81%86%E3%82%84%E3%82%891%E7%B3%BB%E3%81%AB%20%E5%8A%9B%E3%82%92%E5%85%A5%E3%82%8C%E3%81%9A%E3%81%AB2)). As a result, the long-anticipated official release of version 1.9 was effectively shelved, and the message "prioritize 2.0 over the 1 series" was shared with the community.

Additionally, **discussions on PHP8 compatibility and the future of FuelPHP** are sporadically held on the official forum. For instance, at the end of 2022, in response to a user's inquiry about "the status of PHP8.1 compatibility and the desire for an official release of 1.9," a major developer responded, "it's about time to release 1.9" ([php 8.1 - FuelPHP forums](https://fuelphp.com/forums/discussion/15418/php-8-1/p1#:~:text=Hi%2C%20Any%20update%20on%20php8,Harro%20December%202022)). However, as mentioned earlier, the official version 1.9 has not been released to date. In addition, Harro has posted a **“Sneak peek”** on the forum, introducing the progress of 2.0 and sharing the design policy (such as the introduction of a PSR-compliant DI container and a review of the static class structure) with the community ([Sneak peak... - FuelPHP forums](https://fuelphp.com/forums/discussion/15435/sneak-peak-/p1#:~:text=Slowly%20starting%20work%20on%20a,controller%2C%20config%2C%20routing%2C%20views)). From these discussions, it can be inferred that 2.0 is planned to significantly change the architecture from the current FuelPHP, serving to provide guidance for existing users.

Overall, although official information dissemination is limited, **small updates and policies are intermittently shared on forums and social media**. Until a major official announcement (such as the release announcement of FuelPHP 2.0) is made, small forum posts and information exchanges within the community will be the main means of understanding the current situation.
