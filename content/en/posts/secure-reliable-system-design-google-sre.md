[Building Secure and Reliable Systems - Safe System Design, Implementation, and Maintenance According to Google SRE](https://amzn.to/3yBLnNy) was read.

I focused on the first half, so here are my notes.

- Security and reliability are emergent properties; they are not simply implemented but arise from multiple factors (such as mechanisms, testing, and verification states) during deployment and operation.
- It is advisable to consider security and reliability from the early stages of a project. As the project continues, it is likely to accumulate more risks, so early consideration is recommended.
- The understandability of a system (whether engineers can make accurate inferences) depends on its complexity or management (≈ design). Understandable systems are deeply related to reliability and security.

I used to think that SRE and security did not have a close relationship, but reading this book changed my mind.

Although I am not primarily engaged in SRE, I want to read more books related to SRE. In particular, I want to read "SRE: Site Reliability Engineering". There were many references to it in this book, indicating a high relevance.

While this book teaches a mindset, I felt that there are parts that are difficult to grasp practically due to organizational size and structure.
