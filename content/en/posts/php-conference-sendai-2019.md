# TL; DR
Report on attending PHP Conference Sendai 2019.

# Test-Driven Approach to Solve Testing Difficulties
- [Test-Driven Development Approach to Solve Testing Difficulties at PHP Conference Sendai 2019](https://speakerdeck.com/hgsgtk/tesutokaxin-iwojie-jue-surutesutoqu-dong-kai-fa-falseahuroti-at-phpkanhuarensuxian-tai-2019)

- Design decisions from discomfort
 - I felt that when using an approach where tests are written after implementation, I would sense discomfort in the design when I found it hard to write tests. However, with TDD, I think it requires some acclimatization.

# Efforts in Legacy PHP Application Replacement Projects
- [Efforts in Legacy PHP Application Replacement Projects / phpconsen2019](https://speakerdeck.com/blue_goheimochi/phpconsen2019)

- I thought the legacy level was "overwhelming" and it seemed hard to stay motivated...
- Even if it’s not currently "overwhelming" legacy, if left unattended, it will become so in the future. I vowed to actively maintain it when I sense the smell of legacy.

# Techniques for Preventing Recurrence
- [Techniques for Preventing Recurrence / #phpconsen](https://speakerdeck.com/sota1235/number-phpconsen)
- I want to adopt the mindset that "system failures are learning opportunities."
- I thought it was good that the team conducted reviews of recurrence prevention measures across teams.
  - There’s no need to limit it to just the most relevant team; I felt that insights should be shared asynchronously.

# Review Culture in the 6th Year of a Startup
- [Review Culture in the 6th Year of a Startup.pdf](https://speakerdeck.com/tsuyoshi/sutatoatuhu6nian-mu-falserehiyuwen-hua-536de593-7b18-48c0-bfdd-6a59581384b6)
- Our company’s review culture
- I thought reviews are refined to fit the members of the team.
- Reviews are essentially communication, and they can vary greatly depending on the team members, which I find interesting. On the other hand, if we don’t seek discipline in communication, it can directly impact members' motivation and product quality. I want to work towards creating a good culture.
- There were relatively many questions.

# Mastering PHP Middleware
- [Mastering PHP Middleware](https://speakerdeck.com/hiro_y/phpfalsemiddlewarewo-shi-ikonasutameni)
- In my opinion, it was the best and most understandable presentation.
  - It was easy to understand how closures add behavior.
- I didn’t know much about PSR-15…!

# Trying to Implement PSR
- [Trying to Implement PSR](https://niconare.nicovideo.jp/watch/kn3830)
- I became interested in learning by implementing PSR.

# Networking Event
- The networking event LT was interesting.
- I was able to talk with several speakers.
- I felt I was able to talk more closely with many people compared to the last conference in Tokyo.
- Many people mentioned they would also attend LaraCon, so I look forward to seeing them again at the next conference.

# Impressions
I attended for two nights and three days. I felt I should have arrived a bit earlier since there seemed to be interactions among participants from the day before.

Since it was the first time in Sendai, I think the organizers had a lot of challenges, but the content was so good that the participation fee of 500 yen felt too cheap.

It seemed like there was an equal mix of local and traveling participants, with many familiar faces coming from Tokyo. I thought I was actively talking to various people, but perhaps due to time constraints, I regretted not being able to talk much with those who participated locally.
