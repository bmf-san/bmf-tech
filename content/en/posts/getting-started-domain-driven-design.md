[Getting Started with Domain-Driven Design: Practical Techniques for Linking Software Implementation and Business Strategy](https://amzn.to/4rE95PF) is a book I read. This book discusses the concepts and implementation patterns for applying DDD's strategic and tactical design in practice. It systematically covers topics from identifying subdomains, designing bounded contexts, aggregates, domain events, to event sourcing, and carefully explains when to use and avoid each pattern. It also delves into the relationship with microservices and event-driven architecture, making it a distinctive bridge from DDD introduction to practice.
