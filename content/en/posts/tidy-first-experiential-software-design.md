[Tidy First? ―Experiential Software Design for Individuals](https://amzn.to/4iXbNMp) is a quick read at only 130 pages.

It prompts you to consider whether to 'tidy up' from the perspectives of cost, revenue, coupling, and cohesion.

I found the arguments to be concise and easy to understand.

Personally, I think estimating costs and revenues is challenging, and it tests one's skills as a technician.

In particular, I found several chapters that present ideas from a monetary economic approach to be interesting.
