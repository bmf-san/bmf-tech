[Jobs Theory: The Mechanism of Consumption That Makes Innovation Predictable](https://amzn.to/3To9WEi) was read.

Engineers are creatures that solve problems, but there are times when we must sincerely confront what problems users want to solve.

I thought this was a theory I wanted to keep in mind and refer to during such times.

## Word Memo
- Job
  - The problem the customer wants to solve
- Jobs Theory
  - Explains what customers buy and what they do not buy
- Jobs to be Done
  - The progress customers try to achieve in their given situation
    - Customers do not consume just to consume; they consume to solve "something."
- Hiring a Job
  - A metaphor for consumption, but consumption is for achieving progress
- Little Hire
  - How to use what has been bought
  - Innovation requires analysis of Little Hire
  - Something like experiential value (as I interpret it...)
- Big Hire
  - What to buy
  - The data companies collect often tends to focus on this Big Hire
- Subjectivity of Data
  - Objectively appearing data contains the subjectivity of its creator
  - Data should be used to understand correlations, while causal relationships should be captured through Jobs Theory

## Thoughts
Understanding the user's "job" is probably not an easy task.

There was a discussion in the latter part about how defining jobs can help organizations function well, which I found quite interesting.

Given the nature of the engineering profession, it is easy to focus on technology, but this book reminded me that we should cultivate more business sense and theory.

There are various related topics, such as "Users do not know what they want" and "What customers really wanted... (the tire swing)," which made me feel some commonality.
