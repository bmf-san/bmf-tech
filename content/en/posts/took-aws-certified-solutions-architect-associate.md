# Overview
I passed the AWS Certified Solutions Architect - Associate exam, so I wanted to document what I worked on.

# Background
About myself.
I am in my fifth year as a software engineer.
I use AWS at my company and have opportunities to work with it regularly, but I haven't done much in terms of design. (I want to.)

# Motivation
I realized that my knowledge of cloud computing was insufficient (I felt that I had a limited toolbox when trying to think of architectures using the cloud, and there were services I was using with a shallow understanding, etc.), so I thought I should create an opportunity to study properly.

I decided to take the exam because I felt that it would provide valuable learning that is beneficial not just for memorization but also for practical work.

# What I Studied
- [Qwicklabs](https://www.qwiklabs.com/?locale=ja)
  - I think I took most of the AWS courses, excluding specialized ones (like practical solutions for machine learning).
  - I worked on this more to actually get hands-on experience with AWS services rather than just for exam preparation.
    - It took quite a bit of time.
- [AWS Well-Architected Framework](https://wa.aws.amazon.com/wellarchitected/2020-07-02T19-33-23/index.ja.html)
  - This is something I felt I should read first.
- Various materials
  - [AWS Whitepapers and Guides](https://aws.amazon.com/jp/whitepapers/?whitepapers-main.sort-by=item.additionalFields.datePublished&whitepapers-main.sort-order=desc&awsf.whitepapers-flag=*all&awsf.whitepapers-content-type=*all&awsf.whitepapers-content-category=*all&awsf.whitepapers-global-methodology=*all&awsf.whitepapers-business-category=*all&awsf.whitepapers-tech-category=*all&awsf.whitepapers-industries=*all)
  - [AWS Hands-On Materials](https://aws.amazon.com/jp/aws-jp-introduction/aws-jp-webinar-hands-on/)
  - [AWS Service-Specific Materials](https://aws.amazon.com/jp/aws-jp-introduction/aws-jp-webinar-service-cut/)
     - I focused on downloading PDFs related to the Associate level or those I was interested in.
     - I read about 100 PDFs. It was tough...
       - There was quite a lot, so I skimmed through many of them.
  - If focusing on exam preparation, I feel that service introduction materials alone might be sufficient, but it seems better to prioritize reading FAQs and documentation over these.
- AWS Certified Solutions Architect - Associate Practice
  - An official AWS mock exam. It has few questions and takes about 30 minutes.
  - It felt more like a check of understanding.
- [Amazon AWS Free Practice Questions](https://awsjp.com/exam/saa-sap/c/index.html)
  - A site that is publicly available for free.
  - I solved all of them.
- [Udemy SAA-C02 AWS Certified Solutions Architect Associate Mock Exam (6 sets, 390 questions)](https://www.udemy.com/course/aws-knan/learn/quiz/4699792#overview)
  - A collection of higher-difficulty questions.
  - I solved all of them.
  - The content is educational and helps with exam familiarity.
- [Training Library](https://www.aws.training/LearningLibrary?query=&filters=Language%3A2&from=0&size=15&sort=_score)
  - I only worked on the ones that caught my interest.
    - The AWS Well-Architected Framework (Japanese)
    - Subnets, Gateways, and Route Tables Explained (Japanese) (Japanese dubbed version)

# Study Period
About 4 months in total.

I was busy studying mainly in the month leading up to the exam. I reviewed a lot of the areas I didn't understand while working on mock exams.

# What I Did on Exam Day
On the day of the exam, I took it at a test center in Shibuya.

I felt that online testing seemed cumbersome to prepare for, so I decided to take the exam at a test center where the environment was set up.

Before the exam time, I was at a café near the test center, reviewing mock exams and rereading reference books.

I had a good sense of the pace of answering questions from the [Udemy SAA-C02 AWS Certified Solutions Architect Associate Mock Exam](https://www.udemy.com/course/aws-knan/learn/quiz/4699792#overview), so I was able to answer smoothly without feeling rushed.

The difficulty of the questions felt easier than the Udemy mock exam but harder than the official AWS 30-minute mock exam (AWS Certified Solutions Architect - Associate Practice).

After answering all questions with review flags, I used the remaining time to review thoroughly and utilized all my answering time.

I changed my answers for about 2-3 questions during the review. Reviewing is important.

It's nice to know the results immediately after finishing all the questions (though the official notification comes later).

I want to review the questions I got wrong, but it seems there won't be an opportunity for that.

# Changes After Taking the Exam
I feel like I can confidently say I understand the basics of AWS now.

# Thoughts
For studying to pass, I think just cycling through reference books, Udemy, documentation, and FAQs is sufficient. I believe that alone can be educational.

I think it's better to set the exam date in advance and plan your study accordingly.

Since the exam date can be chosen flexibly, it's easy to postpone it casually.
I ended up delaying my exam date by about 1-2 months from my original plan because I was trying to study a lot of things.

Next, I want to take the Professional exam, but I plan to tackle the GCP exam first.
