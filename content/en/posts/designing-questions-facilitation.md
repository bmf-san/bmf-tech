[Designing Questions: Facilitating Creative Dialogue](https://amzn.to/49fe9lE) is a book I read.

It discusses how to design questions, specifically how to formulate and think about them.

I believe that the source of ideas and the essence of problem-solving lie in questions, and I feel daily that the quality of questions directly correlates with the quality of outcomes.

This book also emphasizes that innovation begins with questions, stimulating creativity.

It teaches that discovering questions is just as important as designing them, showing how to elicit questions about things previously unseen, unimagined, unconsidered, or not held as values.

I think you can learn about the depth of questions and hints on how to seek outcomes that exceed expectations.
