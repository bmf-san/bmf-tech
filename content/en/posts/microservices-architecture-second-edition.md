[Microservices Architecture, 2nd Edition](https://amzn.to/3RRUcJV) is a must-read for those working with or considering microservices.

The book touches well on the architectural characteristics (non-functional requirements) needed for distributed systems, making it suitable for readers not solely focused on microservices.

If you want a quick overview of the concept of microservices, the first chapter is well-organized and worth referencing.

While there are some overlaps with monoliths, the book primarily delves into microservices, so it shouldn't be too bothersome.

It doesn't delve much into the implementation of microservices.

It discusses the concepts of shared ownership and strong ownership proposed by Martin Fowler, which are important in the design of organizational and system architecture, serving as one of the connections between organization and system.
