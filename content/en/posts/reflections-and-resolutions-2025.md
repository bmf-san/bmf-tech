# Private
I spent most of my time catching up on design-related topics. I read extensively on architecture and coding.

My interest in DDD and organizational/team management also increased, so I was catching up in those areas as well.

This year was particularly significant for the advancement of AI, and I was considering career development in light of that. However, I concluded that it would not be a problem to continue focusing on the areas I have been interested in (system design and soft skills), so I invested my discretionary time in those pursuits.

I managed to read about 30 books this year. I have been able to increase the number of books I read compared to previous years, so I aim to reach about 50 books a year by finding ways to improve my reading habits. I feel increasingly aware of my knowledge gaps each year, and with the evolution of AI, the usefulness of accumulating knowledge seems to be inflating. I want to continue thinking about efficient ways to input information. There are various improvements I can make, especially in how I read books. I tend to try to read every page due to my personality, so there is room for improvement in my reading approach. I can also utilize AI for information gathering, which still has potential for efficiency.

Regarding output, I continued to engage in activities like giving talks and creating OSS, similar to previous years. While I say it’s the same as before, there has been a change in my mindset. I have become more interested in increasing my visibility. Previously, I engaged in such activities (blogs and presentations) from the perspective of my own growth, but I have started to feel that the positive impact of visibility on my career and growth cannot be overlooked. I believe it will be an important factor in the career I aspire to, so I want to continue to be active in this regard.

# Work
> I may not be able to feel my growth.

I wrote something like this last year, and I feel the same way this year. I feel that I am gaining experience and that it is beneficial for my career, but the expectations for abstraction and results in my work have changed over the years, which may diminish my sense of growth. My primary concern is the impact of results rather than the process. Additionally, it takes time for the things I am working on to lead to significant results, so that factor remains.

This year, I was promoted, which was the most significant event.

# Reflection on Aspirations
My aspiration was to "choose my weapons."

> I feel that generative AI as a supplementary weapon changes the way I choose my weapons.

I think that was mostly true. I feel that I have made significant progress in the idea of choice and concentration regarding how I spend my time as a software engineer. I believe I am spending the most time gaining insights into architectural design from various perspectives. Additionally, I am spending time on soft skills, such as how to improve communication and thinking skills, which may seem ordinary.

# Aspirations for Next Year
I plan to make "de-AI" my aspiration.

This means not to avoid using AI, but to not rely on it too much.

My biggest reflection this year is that I relied on AI and delegated my thinking to it. I believe the efficiency and total amount of input have likely increased, and I think output has also increased accordingly, but I have many concerns about whether I improved the quality or whether it became part of me. I also feel that my thinking ability has declined. I reflect that I have used AI in a very poor way, so I want to improve my use of AI next year. I have various specific ideas, but as a simple constraint for improvement, I will consciously follow the rule of not using AI casually.
