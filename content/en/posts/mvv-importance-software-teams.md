In software development, discussing whether something is "technically correct" is important. However, that alone is not enough. Technical correctness can easily change due to trends in technology, execution environments, or shifts in business phases.

While having a logical structure is a prerequisite, in a rapidly changing development environment, it is essential for teams to have a consistent evaluation framework based on a foundational "value system".

## Redefining MVV in Development Teams

There are various interpretations of MVV (Mission, Vision, Values), but in this article, we will redefine it based on Peter Drucker's organizational theory to function at the practical level of software development teams. It is important to focus not on grand slogans at the management level, but on "what kind of specialized group we are, and to whom we provide what in our domain".

- **Mission**: "What do we consider as our contribution?" What kind of changes does the team bring to the target audience that we deliver value to?
- **Vision**: "What is our ideal state?" The image of the team or product we want to realize as a result of continuously fulfilling our mission.
- **Values**: "What do we believe is right?" The guiding principles for determining priorities in daily design decisions and troubleshooting.

## Defining Team Persona: How to Think

MVV should not be decided unilaterally by the leader. To define the team as a single "persona", a process to synchronize the understanding of all members is necessary.

1. **Ask, "Who are our customers (users)?"**: Who are the people directly using our code or systems? Clarify what challenges they face and how the team’s "mission" is to solve them.
2. **Understanding Domain and Context**: Articulate what role the domain the team faces requires from the team persona.
3. **Extracting Criteria for Judgment**: Reflect on past design decisions and extract the common values of "what we believed was right" in our actions.

The "team persona" defined through this process should be an identity that is inherited even when members change. Moreover, it is important that MVV is not fixed once established, but flexibly adjusted according to changes in circumstances.

## Maintaining Consistency of the "Team Persona"

A team is a collection of individuals, but it should function as a single "persona". The persona of the team is defined by the domain and context it faces.

Even if there are changes in members, if there is no consistency in the value and behavior the team provides, trust from the surroundings cannot be obtained. Establishing MVV is essentially the process of defining the "identity of the team persona". With this axis in place, even with changes in personnel, the approach to the domain can be inherited, allowing the team to maintain consistency.

## Sharing "Thoughts" to Improve Decision-Making Accuracy and Speed

In design and technology selection, the final decision is made by the team’s "thoughts". Of course, various judgment factors such as logical correctness, quantitative data, or qualitative judgment criteria are important and should not be overlooked.

However, when multiple "logically correct options" are presented, discussions can become stagnant without a common thought (Value).

- "Should we prioritize delivery speed and accept debt, or maintain robust design?"
- "Should we optimize for specific use cases, or create generic abstractions anticipating future reuse?"

By having a common understanding of thoughts through MVV, decision-making as a team persona becomes smoother, preventing unproductive discussions.

## Promoting Autonomy for Continuous Improvement

An autonomous team refers to a team that can make decisions and complete tasks as a team persona.

It is important that individual members do not act separately, but that the team as a subject (persona) constantly questions, "Is our current development process optimal?" and "Is the direction we are aiming for correct?", and continues to update itself flexibly. MVV serves as a compass for this autonomous improvement cycle.

## Operations to Prevent MVV from Becoming a Mere Formality

To make MVV a living guideline, it is useful to integrate it into daily routines.

- Display it in places that are frequently seen, such as Slack Canvas or KPT reflection boards.
- Ensure that team members can articulate the team’s MVV in their own words to potential hires.
- Develop a habit of referring back to MVV as a basis for decision-making when in doubt.

## Example of MVV in a Platform Development Team

Based on the author's experience, here is a model case of MVV in a team responsible for platform engineering. The composition may change depending on the team's focus points.

|     Pattern     |                   Mission                    |                           Vision                           |           Values           |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Developer Experience Focus | Abstract complexity to create an environment where developers can immerse themselves in implementation | A world where anyone can instantly deliver ideas as software | User-centered design / Zero hassle experience |
| Safety and Control Focus | Make security the default while balancing freedom and control | A state where all engineers can accelerate without conscious effort | Safety by default / Ensuring transparency |
| Reliability and Scale Focus | Maintain a robust foundation that can withstand 100x scale | Infrastructure that allows all products to enjoy maximum scale at minimal cost | Prioritizing reliability |

## Conclusion

Engineering is a world of logic, but it is the team's will that decides how that logic is directed. Because technical correctness fluctuates with the environment, teams should define MVV to maintain a common understanding as a team persona, promoting consistent yet flexible development. By using the established words as a compass, it becomes a shortcut to maximizing the value of the product as the team continues to refine its existence.
