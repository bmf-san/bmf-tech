I have resigned from my current job after 5 years and 5 months.

It was the company where I worked the longest in my career.

Before joining, I only had experience as an application engineer, and I believe that the experiences and challenges I faced at this company greatly supported my growth.

I had the valuable experience of witnessing the company's IPO phase and closely experiencing the growth of the organization and business.

In my personal life, I started living alone for the first time, met my wife, moved in together, got married, had a child, and took parental leave, marking various life stages. I went from being in my 20s to suddenly finding myself in my 30s.

The company supported these changes in my personal life as well.

I am truly grateful to the company that greatly supported both my work and personal life.

I was very fortunate to have enjoyable work and to be blessed with respectable bosses and colleagues.

When considering resignation, there were many good people who made it hard to leave.

I would be delighted if our relationships continue or if there are opportunities to work together again in the future.

It is a company I am very attached to in my life, and I still love the business and products, but I decided to resign because I found a new challenge.

Thank you very much for the nearly five and a half years.
