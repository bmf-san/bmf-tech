[Kaizen Journey](https://amzn.to/44eW18J) is a book I read, and I wanted to jot down my thoughts.

# Thoughts
I had the impression that the story format was both easy and difficult to read, but upon finishing, I felt it was a clear way to experience the concept of "crossing borders."

The Agile practices presented were not new to me, as I had learned about them elsewhere.

However, this book strongly conveys the purpose and significance of "crossing borders" rather than focusing on the details of those practices.

Reflecting on my own experiences, I have thought about what kinds of improvements are necessary for a team to enhance its results beyond its own boundaries and contribute to the organization.

I feel that "crossing borders" is an important keyword in the pursuit of results in software projects, such as achieving greater outcomes, delivering results faster, and making it easier to achieve results.

I believe this book addresses themes like how to expand the field of value delivery from an individual to a team, and from a team to the entire organization, improving development through process enhancements.

I read this book without any prior knowledge, but it might have been easier to read if I had looked at the Appendix first.

The story in this book is set in a contract development company, but I think those with experience in business companies might find the content more relatable. (There might be some bias and prejudice based on my own experiences...)

So, I wonder if Sodegaura could have collaborated with Ejima and others from the very beginning, haha.
