# I Participated and Spoke at PHP Conference Fukuoka 2019
I attended the PHP Conference Fukuoka for the first time.

I had been unsuccessful in the CFP until now, but this year I was able to be accepted, so I participated as a speaker.

This was my second time attending a conference outside of Tokyo, following the PHP Conference Sendai.

Here are my presentation materials.
[Creating URL Routing in PHP](https://speakerdeck.com/bmf_san/phpdeurlruteinguwozi-zuo-suru)

This was a topic I spoke about at PHPerKaigi, but I presented it again with some progress and feedback taken into account. 
(This year's PHP Conference CFP is only about routing. Since this has become a theme I will continue to engage with this year, I hope to update it little by little.)

[Participated and Spoke at PHPerKaigi 2019](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/PHPerKaigi2019%E3%81%AB%E5%8F%82%E5%8A%A0%E3%83%BB%E7%99%BB%E5%A3%87%E3%81%97%E3%81%A6%E3%81%8D%E3%81%BE%E3%81%97%E3%81%9F)

I have discussed the content of the slides in past articles, so I will skip that here.

At the end of my talk, I briefly mentioned that I am currently reviewing the data structure and algorithms for routing, studying string search trees, and working on creating routing in Golang as originally intended. (I haven't committed anything yet, but I have an image of the implementation...)

# The Day Before
I arrived in Fukuoka a day early for a 2-night, 3-day plan.

I took a week off and was in Miyako Island until the day before. It was quite an aggressive schedule for me to return to Haneda and then go to Fukuoka the next day.

The day before, I participated in packing novelty items for the conference preparation, but there were so many helpers that the throughput was too high, and it ended quickly.

I felt that many people with the spirit of supporting the conference, regardless of whether they were staff or participants, were likely to attend this event.

# On the Day
## Main Sessions
Here are the sessions I attended on the day.

[What Should We Communicate in PHP Developer Interviews? #phpconfuk](https://speakerdeck.com/kurikazu/phperfalsecai-yong-mian-jie-de-pu-rahahe-wotutaeaubekika-number-phpconfuk)

I am not in a hiring position, but I thought it was important for job seekers to know about the hiring perspective, so I listened.

I can relate to the story of being asked about motivation during a casual interview...

In my experience, I have received selection results after a casual interview.

I believe that casual interviews can effectively serve as selection materials, but I think sincerity should be maintained on both sides.

Borrowing the words from the last slide, "I want to meet you energetically."

[Trying Clean Architecture with Laravel #phpconfuk](https://www.slideshare.net/ShoheiOkada/laravel-phpconfuk-152500600)

Recently, I have been working on implementing clean architecture in Go, so I was interested and listened.

I thought I might try to express business logic in natural language as well.

[What I Did to Remove 200,000 Lines in 3 Months](https://speakerdeck.com/naopr/3keyue-de20mo-xing-woxiao-sutameniyatutakoto)

I was curious about the background and spoke with the presenter at ATS.

Instead of forming a project team in the context of debt resolution, it seems that the individual took the initiative and worked on it grassroots-style, and I was impressed by their motivation.

They focused on removing code based on whether it was used or not, rather than deprecated code.

If you address deprecated code, refactoring becomes necessary, which can be quite challenging...

When splitting services or upgrading language and package versions, it can be futile to address unnecessary code, so I think it’s important to have the courage to start with just listing them.

[Password-less Web Applications Created with WebAuthn.](https://speakerdeck.com/kensukemasatani/password-less-web-applications-created-with-webauthn)

I thought this was a trending topic in authentication, so I listened.

The idea that humans do not generate passwords was quite enlightening.

There were many other sessions I wanted to see on the day, but unfortunately, they overlapped with my talk or I couldn't attend due to time constraints. I plan to thoroughly review the slides later.

[Execution of PHP Functions and Their Measurement](https://www.slideshare.net/shinjiigarashi/php-152597215)
I thought it would only be about profilers, but it started with a deep dive into processing systems, which was very interesting, though I lost track halfway...

I feel like I somewhat understand the concepts and mechanisms, but I lacked the skills in C language or assembly to follow the actual processing system implementation.

I aspire to create a compiler someday (with determination).

## Lightning Talks

I watched all the lightning talks! (I will skip the links...)

Although time ran out midway, I was most interested in [Shall We Start Competitive Programming? / PHP Conference Fukuoka 2019](https://speakerdeck.com/nabeen/php-conference-fukuoka-2019). (I reviewed all the slides later.)

I have just started studying algorithms through creating routing, but I believe that the thinking of data structures and algorithms can become the foundational strength of programming, so I often think that whether or not to use algorithms is not the point; rather, the way of thinking and patterns can be applied in practice.

I gained motivation to continue studying properly!

Also, I was impressed that four people from a certain company participated, as always.

# Reflections on Participating in PHP Conference Fukuoka
I had a total of five toasts during the pre-party, the Yells Festival, the third party, the networking party on the day, and the Yells Festival (second time), which allowed me to deepen my connections with various people.

I truly believe that this is the same for study groups and other events, but when horizontal connections are made, you can hear stories that are not usually public and discuss with people who have similar challenges and questions, which is great.

I feel that conferences especially provide opportunities for such connections, so I want to continue participating actively in the future.

Thank you to the staff of the PHP Conference and the participants who attended the sessions.

I definitely want to participate again next year! It was fun!!
