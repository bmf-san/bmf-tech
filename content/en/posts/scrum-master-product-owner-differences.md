# Overview

# What is a Scrum Master?
## Responsibilities
- Responsible for establishing and ensuring the effectiveness of Scrum
  - Ensure that the Scrum theory and processes are understood by the Scrum team so that Scrum is practiced correctly
  - Enable the Scrum team to improve processes based on the Scrum framework

## Roles
- Improve team performance
  - Enhance processes
  - Remove impediments
- Facilitate Scrum events
- Visualize work
- Achieve goals
- Not a manager or administrator

# What is a Product Owner?
## Responsibilities
- Responsible for maximizing the value of the product
- Responsible for managing the product backlog

## Roles
- Clarify the product vision
- Set product goals
- Establish release plans
- Manage the budget
- Create the product backlog
- Hold authority over the prioritization of product backlog items
- Ensure the product backlog is complete
- Collaborate with stakeholders

# Differences Between Scrum Master and Product Owner
The Scrum Master is concerned with the process (How), while the Product Owner is focused on the product's value and vision (What and Why).

In practicing Scrum, the Scrum Master understands Scrum, evangelizes it to the team, and facilitates process improvement, while the Product Owner makes decisions to maximize the product's value.

# Personal Thoughts
From my personal perspective, I do not recommend combining the roles of Scrum Master and Product Owner, but I feel that combining the Scrum Master and developer roles is more acceptable. However, I think it is challenging to fully fulfill the responsibilities of each position. In cases where there are not enough resources to have a dedicated Scrum Master, what about having a Scrum Master (dedicated, responsible for Scrum Master duties, possibly serving multiple teams) plus a developer who acts as a Scrum Master (a leadership role assisting the Scrum Master)? This seems similar to the environment I have experienced.
