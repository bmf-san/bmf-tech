# Publishing My Trello and Spreadsheet

~~I tried setting my Trello, which visualizes tasks I do in my private time, and the spreadsheet that manages my Oreo-less print measurement data to public.~~

→ Currently, it is private.

I have been thinking that I want to keep my activities as an engineer as open as possible, so I decided to publish them.

I might have a feeling that before I became an engineer and even now, I want to know what other engineers are studying and thinking about, and before I can see others' work, I should publish my own.

# Reviewing Task Management
I have been visualizing tasks in Trello for about 2-3 years, but this year I changed my operation to do labeling, task division, and estimation more properly, and started measuring it with Oreo-less print.

The reason for this change is that I wanted to make my skill improvement more efficient.

Until now, my operation was just like a simple Todo list, without much awareness of priority, and I was tackling tasks as I wanted.

In situations where I wanted to do this and that, I often ended up completing only 1 or 2 tasks, which was a disappointing task handling situation. I felt it was not good for how I used my time and for my mental health.

Amidst this casual operation, I loosely experienced Scrum development and realized daily that there is not enough time in life, so I felt the need to operate more efficiently and in a balanced way, which led me to reconsider my operations.

# New Task Management Policy
I will measure how many roughly estimated tasks I can accomplish within a sprint (1 week) that I set.

The estimated work hours are not in rough time but in days. I thought it would be better to estimate tasks in time units for accuracy, but private time has more interruptions (urgent matters, household chores, etc.) than work time, so it can be flexible but not really.

Therefore, instead of estimating in time units, I decided to use days to allow for a larger buffer.

I treated this time estimation like story points and visualized how many tasks I could accomplish in a week and the total estimated score in the Spreadsheet.

Since there are only 7 days in a week, if the total score is 7 or more, I can get a rough result that I worked hard that week.

The purpose of starting this measurement is not to increase the amount of tasks completed within the sprint (throughput), but to stabilize the number of tasks I can accomplish within the sprint.

While it is good to achieve more than a week's worth of results in one week, aiming for that every week is not a realistic goal. I recognize that maximizing throughput in my unstable private time is a challenge I should focus on in the future.

This week, I have various plans for dining out, so I think I can handle this number of tasks → I did it! By properly cycling this, I want to use my time efficiently and improve my skills nicely.

# Thoughts
I believe the purpose of a product sprint review is to maximize the value of the product, but I think I can consider the purpose of an individual task sprint review (just measurement) to be maximizing my skills, so I want to try continuing this operation for a year. (The detailed operational policy may change during that time.)

# Aside
About task creation.

I have a rough idea of things I want to do, things I should do, and things that would be good to do, so I try to create tasks for them.

I don’t create tasks on specific days; I create them whenever I think of something, whether at work or out.

Things I want to do are those that pique my technical curiosity.
Things I should do are necessary for work or things that will cause problems if not done.
Things that would be good to do are those that seem beneficial in the future or might impact my career. Investment activities.

I believe that the overlap of these three types of tasks is the best for me, but I feel like I am mostly doing what I want.
