[The Thinking Method of World-Class Engineers](https://amzn.to/3UhEw42) is a book I read a while ago, so here are my notes.

Much of what is written in this book is something I have heard or learned somewhere before, so personally, I didn't find it very novel. However, there were a couple of points I wanted to keep in mind.

One is the idea of being 'Belazy.' This is the mindset of maximizing value with less time.

This is a mindset that can be practiced alone, but I think it's even more important for the surrounding environment to also be 'Belazy.'

The other point is about taking time to understand. No matter how smart someone is perceived to be, they often read the same text multiple times to understand it, rather than trying to grasp it in one go. (There may be people who can understand things in one shot or have a quick understanding speed.)

When trying to understand something, it is common to avoid repetition due to time constraints. However, I want to be more conscious of taking the necessary time for parts that I want to deepen my understanding of or that are difficult to grasp.
