[How to Write Code That Fits in Your Brain - Rules of Thumb and Techniques to Avoid Complexity and Ensure Sustainability](https://amzn.to/4gD7NxX) was read.

From the title, I imagined it might include cognitive science discussions reminiscent of [programmer brain](https://bmf-tech.com/posts/%e3%83%97%e3%83%ad%e3%82%b0%e3%83%a9%e3%83%9e%e8%84%b3), but that was not the case.

This book discusses practical practices for writing code that fits in your brain's memory.

Overall, I found many parts relatable based on my own experiences. The author seems to have a background not from a computer science department but has built a career as a software engineer, which gives a sense of experience cultivated in the field.

Regarding the content, I felt it was somewhat fragmented.

The discussion on fractal architecture felt a bit brief, so I would love to read a book by the author focused on the theme of fractal architecture.
