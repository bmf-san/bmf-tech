[Balancing Software Design Coupling: Principles of Modularity for Sustainable Growth](https://amzn.to/4q8HN2Y) is a rare book focused on coupling, providing a theoretical understanding of how to manage the complexity of architectural design.

I believe that managing complexity is crucial for determining the lifespan of architecture, and this book allows for a deep exploration of coupling as an analytical perspective for that purpose.

Having read it only once, I found it difficult to fully absorb, so I want to keep it on hand for reference like a dictionary when designing. The perspectives on coupling are very well organized, which I think will be helpful for design analysis.

Research on coupling is not a recent topic; it has been studied for a long time. However, the difficulty of managing it seems to be a perpetual challenge for software engineers. This likely stems from the essence of software engineering being rooted in the management of complexity.
